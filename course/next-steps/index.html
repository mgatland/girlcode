Next Steps after Girl Code

So you've finished Girl Code... here are some ideas for what to do next.

Learn for a project

You'll find it easier to learn if you are working towards a goal - maybe making a personal portfolio website, or a page for a club you belong to.

Revise the course

You can revise the activities we did during the course. A good way to do this is to make your own app, using the course as a guide.

All our slides and activities from the first half of the course are available here: http://girlcode.co.nz/course.

The issues we worked on in the second half can be found here: https://github.com/girlcodeakl.

  • Click on the name your team project on the list
  • Click on the 'Issues' tab near the top
  • This view only shows open (unfinished) issues. Click on the 'Closed' option to see the closed or finished issues.

To make your own app, create a new GitHub repository - go to the GitHub homepage, make sure you're logged in to your account, and look for a 'New' button.

All of the products and services we used are free, so whenever we used a Girl Code account (like for Heroku and MLab) you can create your own free account and use that instead.

Online activities

Free online activities are a good follow-up to Girl Code because our course is quite chaotic (like a real workplace!) but online activities have a slower step-by-step approach that helps make sure you cover all the basics.

We recommend the free courses at Codecademy. Take a look at the courses on HTML + CSS, JavaScript, 'Build a Website', 'Using the Command Prompt', or anything else you're interested in.

Meetups

Auckland has lots of events for tech people. Try searching meetup.com for tech terms like 'Javascript', 'CSS' or 'Node'. Most of these are evening events where someone gives a talk, then there's time to hang out and chat. A few cost money but most are free to attend.

One particularly good event is Refactor, which has talks by women from the NZ tech community. It usually has some pretty amazing speakers.

Full time study in Auckland

Full time study is expensive, but a good way to get into tech.

Enrolling in Dev Academy may be the fastest way to become a work-ready developer.

Getting a university degree is a good way to get into a tech career, but takes at least 3 years! Matthew studied at the University of Auckland.
